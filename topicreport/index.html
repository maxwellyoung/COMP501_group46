Topic: Cybersecurity and AI Security Risks in Open-Source Software

Relevance and Importance:

The proliferation of open-source software (OSS) has revolutionized the tech industry, allowing rapid innovation and collaboration. Many groundbreaking technologies, such as Samsungâ€™s Galaxy series and Google's Android OS, owe their success to the open-source model. Recently, AI-related software has also been open-sourced, with major companies like Google and Microsoft releasing critical AI tools such as TensorFlow to the public. This openness fosters a collaborative environment where developers can build upon existing technologies without starting from scratch, accelerating technological advancements.

However, the widespread adoption of OSS, particularly AI-integrated OSS, introduces significant cybersecurity risks. The open nature of OSS means that anyone, including malicious actors, can access and potentially exploit vulnerabilities. These security concerns are heightened in AI systems due to their complexity and the challenges in monitoring and securing them effectively.

Why We Have Chosen This Topic:

Safety and Privacy:

Protecting sensitive data and ensuring user privacy is paramount in the digital age. Vulnerabilities in OSS can lead to data breaches and privacy violations.

Innovation and Collaboration:

A secure open-source environment encourages continued innovation and collaboration within the global tech community, fostering growth and development.

Regulatory Compliance:

Adhering to cybersecurity best practices helps organizations comply with data protection regulations, avoiding legal issues and penalties.

Sustainable Development:

Ensuring the security and reliability of OSS supports the long-term sustainability of software projects, benefiting both developers and users.

Our team will explore the cybersecurity and AI security risks associated with open-source software, examining the socio-economic implications, potential vulnerabilities, and strategies for mitigation. By understanding and addressing these challenges, we aim to contribute to a safer and more secure open-source community, ultimately supporting the continued growth and adoption of innovative technologies.
