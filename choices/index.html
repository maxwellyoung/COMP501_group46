  • COMP501 Group 46: Cybersecurity and AI in Open-Source Security

Choices

There are two choices to consider that relate to cybersecurity and AI security in open-source software. Firstly, there is the choice of increasing the power of the security tools themselves. In order to fully utilise AI security, knowledge of these tools functionality is necessary in order to ensure safety for its users and lessen the gaps in its security. Open-source software has steps and guidelines established from community feedback, past issues, and tests to minimise security anomalies and further understand the strength and weaknesses of the software.

A survey conducted by Rahman et al. (2023) concluded that among the common and major variation of attacks on AI learning models, the most prevalent are adversarial attacks. To combat this threat, adversarial training approaches are employed. The training involves putting the AI model in adversarial attack scenarios, which helps the model recognise and resist future adversarial attacks.

Additionally, other implements of cyber security such as regular updates and patches and secure coding practices are just as beneficial. Ryan et al. (2023) suggests that one of the main solutions to vulnerable software, is secure coding practices, which as mentioned before is a set of instructions and guides followed by a developer in order to gain the most security out of their software upon completion. Due to AI having the ability to learn and improve from each encounter with security obstacles. The implementation of AI in the cybersecurity world has drastically increased the levels of expectations for modern day cybersecurity. AI security tools is the future, and the first steps towards achieving absolute cybersecurity.

Secondly, another choice to consider regarding cybersecurity and AI security is the use of secure open-source AI software. Secure open-source AI software is an inexpensive alternative for those who lack funds for expensive cybersecurity technology. For instance, some free-to-use software are Apache Spot and DeepExploit which incorporates AI to identify threats and test for vulnerabilities. Both of the software are open-source software therefore opening doors to collaboration, adaptability, and community feedback.

According to Ahmad (2020), the development of open-source software came with benefits such as free alternatives to traditional software, developers having more freedom, and accelerated advancements. Although the security levels of the free-to-use AI security software may not match those with licensing fees, small businesses and developers can be reassured knowing that the open-source nature of their tools allows for continuing development, meaning that their security tool will most likely be future proof.

With the introduction of free-to-use cybersecurity alternatives, large companies such as Darktrace and Crowdstrike Falcon gain competitors, as there are cheaper alternatives to consider. The availability of costless alternatives to cybersecurity levels the playing field between large and small developers and organisations, which promotes a more inclusive approach to cybersecurity.
