  • COMP501 Group 46: Open Source Software

Choices

There are two choices to consider amongst the rest that relate to AI security for open-source software. Firstly, there is the choice of assuring the tools protection. In order to fully utilise AI security, knowledge of the tool's functionality is necessary to ensure safety for users. Open-source software follows steps and guidelines established from community feedback, past issues, and tests to minimise security anomalies and further understand the strength and weaknesses of the software. A survey conducted by Rahman et al. (2023) concluded that among the common and major variation of attacks on AI learning models, the most prevalent are adversarial attacks. To combat this threat, adversarial training approaches are employed. The training involves putting the AI model in adversarial attack scenarios, which helps the model recognise and resist future adversarial attacks. AI security software have the ability to learn, which assists with improvement vastly. Security tools that improve with each obstacle is the first step to gaining absolute security.

Secondly, another choice is to use secure open-source AI software. Secure open-source AI software is an inexpensive alternative for those who lack funds for expensive security technology. For example, software such as Apache Spot and DeepExploit incorporates AI to identify threats and test for vulnerabilities. Both of the software are free-to-use open-source software therefore opening doors to collaboration, adaptability, and community feedback. According to Ahmad (2021), the development of open-source software came with benefits such as free alternatives to traditional software, developers having more freedom, and accelerated advancements. Although they may lack the security levels of those AI security tools that comes with licensing fees, small businesses are able to rest assured knowing that the open-source nature of their tool allows for continuing development. The availability of free alternatives to security software levels the playing field for developers and organisations.
